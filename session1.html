Session 1

An individual and group exercise

I've done some brainstorming in the past, and what I find interesting about brainstorming is that you have to break it down to understand what it means, storm and brain. In my experience and in the course we had, brainstorming is the ability of a group to come up with new creative ideas, to innovate, to think differently. Like with the expression 1+1=3. Moreover, according to me, despite its connotation of freedom, I think that there must be rules such as not criticizing the ideas of others, that all ideas are good to say, even the craziest ones, and that one should not hesitate to take another's idea to make one again. Then you have to clean up all the ideas because in my experience brainstorming is never very aesthetic at the beginning, so you sort out the best ideas to reach a common consensus.
